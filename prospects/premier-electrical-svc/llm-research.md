## Agent Synthesis

### Must-Have Sections (consensus across all 4 models)
- Hero: real photo, location in H1, prominent phone, gold CTA button
- Trust bar immediately below hero: Best of Skagit 3x, A+ BBB, licensed/bonded/insured, years experience
- Services grid (icon cards → individual pages)
- Project showcase / before-and-after
- Testimonials with names, cities, star ratings
- Service area map (county-labeled)
- About teaser with real team photo
- FAQ accordion (3–8 questions)
- Final CTA + minimal contact form (4 fields max)
- Footer: full NAP, license number, city list, quick links

### Design Patterns to Replicate
- Navy/dark base + gold accent (we have this) — highest trust palette for electrical
- Bold condensed sans-serif headings (Montserrat, Oswald — we have DM Sans Bold which works)
- Real photos > stock everywhere; before/after on service pages
- Sticky header with phone number on every page
- Video hero background = 2.6x time on page (nice to have)
- Generous white space between sections

### Industry-Specific Trust Signals for Electrical
1. Google star rating count (100+ reviews = threshold for maximum visibility)
2. L&I license number visible with verification link on every page
3. A+ BBB badge ABOVE THE FOLD, not in footer
4. "Best of Skagit Valley" 3x award in hero badge
5. Specific insurance coverage stated ($2M general liability > generic "insured")
6. Satisfaction guarantee / workmanship warranty
7. Master Electrician credential named explicitly

### Competitor Weaknesses to Exploit (local market)
- No Skagit-based electrician has a dedicated historic home rewiring page
- No local competitor has barn/shop/outbuilding wiring content
- Rhema Electric (Bellingham) is the biggest threat — expanding location pages aggressively
- Most sites have vague service areas ("the region") — Premier must name specific cities
- Most sites bury BBB/awards in footer or About page
- Zero local competitors have a membership/maintenance program (Rhema has it; huge opportunity)
