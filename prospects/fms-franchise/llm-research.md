I am redesigning/modernizing the website for Franchise Marketing Systems (FMS Franchise), a full-service franchise consulting agency founded in 2009. They have:
- 67 in-house franchise consultants
- 900+ brands franchised
- 7 global offices (Alpharetta GA, Boston, Miami, Toronto, Vietnam, Abuja, Barbados)
- Core services: Franchise Development, Franchise Sales, Management & Support, Business Plans, Efficiency Audit, Digital Marketing
- They target business owners who want to franchise their business

I need you to answer these specific questions for designing their new website:

1. What are the MUST-HAVE sections/pages for a B2B franchise consulting firm website? What do the best-in-class franchise consulting websites always include that average ones miss?

2. What are the top 3-5 design patterns from award-winning professional services / B2B consulting websites? (Think visual hierarchy, social proof treatment, CTA placement, etc.)

3. What trust signals are most important to a business owner deciding whether to hire a franchise consultant? List the top 10 in priority order.

4. Who are FMS Franchise's main competitors (FranConnect, Franchise Group, FranChoice, others)? What are their biggest website weaknesses that FMS could do better?

5. What is the typical franchise consulting buyer journey? What questions does a business owner have at each stage, and how should the site answer them?

6. What pages beyond the standard set would be most valuable for a franchise consulting firm? (E.g., ROI calculator, case studies, industry-specific landing pages, state-by-state guides, etc.)

7. What emotional messaging tends to convert B2B clients in the franchise consulting space? What are the core fears, desires, and objections?

Please be specific and actionable.
